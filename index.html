Heavana

A stress-buster game made with Flutter.

The Idea

Heavana was born out of curiosity and fun — what if stress relief could come from something as simple as throwing your phone (safely, of course)? Inspired by old quirky apps like SMTH and Carrot Pop, I wanted to reimagine that playful concept with a modern design, smoother physics, and an experience that actually makes you smile.

Heavana measures how high you throw your phone, then playfully reacts to your action — a mix of humor, physics, and interaction all in one.

Early Design (SMTH 2)

The first prototype, named SMTH 2, was a simple exploration on Figma. It borrowed some ideas from existing apps but aimed to improve on visual structure and usability. I experimented with clean typography, subtle motion, and rounded edges to make the experience feel friendly and lighthearted.

But during review, I realized the design was too similar to copyrighted visuals — which meant I had to start over.

Redesigning From Scratch

Instead of fixing the old, I decided to rebuild Heavana entirely from scratch. The new design embraces a minimal but expressive look — large typography, soft color gradients, and playful spacing. Everything is intentionally rounded to keep the visual energy positive and calming.

It feels like a tribute, not a clone.

The Physics Behind Heavana

Heavana uses Flutter's sensor capabilities — specifically the accelerometer — to track your phone's upward motion.

When you throw the phone upward, the app captures the motion curve and calculates the height using the equation:

h = ½ × g × t²

where g is the gravitational constant and t is the time of flight.

The logic continuously measures acceleration, detects the peak, and then returns the maximum height reached. If the phone isn't thrown, a fun message like "bruhh…" appears instead — because humor matters!

Sound & Interaction

Heavana isn't just about numbers. It's about feel. Every button and event is paired with meme-like sounds — short, funny clips that instantly trigger a reaction.

When a user presses a button, it plays a random meme sound, making the experience unpredictable and engaging.

Design & Vibe

Heavana's interface is designed to match a calm yet playful tone — clean blues, rounded components, and a touch of kinetic movement. The typography is expressive but legible, giving space to both fun and focus.

Closing Thoughts

Heavana is a small experiment in joy — built in Flutter, designed with humor, and powered by curiosity. It's not just a game; it's a reminder that even small interactions can lift your mood — literally.
